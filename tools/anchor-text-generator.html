What is anchor text and why does it matter?

Anchor text is the clickable text in a hyperlink — the visible words that a user sees and clicks. Search engines like Google use anchor text as a strong relevance signal: it tells them what the destination page is about. When 100 websites link to a page using the words "best running shoes", Google interprets that page as highly relevant for the query "best running shoes".

But here is the catch. Since the Penguin update in 2012, Google penalizes sites with unnatural anchor text profiles — typically too many exact-match anchors. A healthy backlink profile must look organic, with most anchors being branded, generic, or naked URLs, and only a small minority being exact match keywords.

The 6 anchor text categories explained

1. Branded anchors

The brand name or domain. runfast.com, RunFast. These are the safest type — every natural backlink profile is dominated by branded anchors. Target: 40-50% of all anchors.

2. Exact match anchors

The exact target keyword. best running shoes. Powerful for ranking but extremely risky in volume. Target: max 5% of all anchors.

3. Partial match anchors

Keyword variations or longer phrases containing the keyword. find the best running shoes here. Natural and safe. Target: 15-25%.

4. LSI / semantic anchors

Related terms and synonyms. top trail runners, marathon footwear guide. Help Google understand topical relevance without overoptimization. Target: 10-20%.

5. Generic anchors

Call-to-action phrases. click here, read more, visit this site. Common in editorial linking. Target: 10-15%.

6. Naked URLs

The bare URL. https://runfast.com. Forum posts and citations often use these. Target: 10-15%.

What is a safe anchor text ratio in 2026?

For most niches, aim for roughly 45% branded, 15% naked URL, 15% generic, 15% partial/LSI, and under 5% exact match. Competitive niches like finance or supplements should be even more conservative.

Can over-optimized anchor text cause a Google penalty?

Yes. Google's Penguin algorithm specifically targets unnatural link patterns, including over-optimized exact-match anchors. Sites that overuse exact match anchors often lose rankings overnight.
