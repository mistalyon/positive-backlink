Anchor Text Categories Explained

The tool classifies every anchor in your list into one of six buckets. Search engines look at the distribution across these buckets to judge whether your link profile looks organically earned or artificially built.

CategoryDefinitionHealthy RangeRed Flag
Exact MatchAnchor equals your target keyword verbatim1–8%> 15%

How the Risk Score Is Calculated

The composite score weights each anchor bucket by deviation from its healthy band, then normalizes to a 0–100 scale. Exact-match overuse counts double because that is the single strongest correlate with Penguin-era algorithmic demotions in the SEO industry.

  • 0–30 (Green): Profile reads as naturally earned. Continue diversifying anchors.
  • 31–60 (Yellow): Mild over-optimization signal. Slow down on commercial anchors, dilute with branded/naked URL links.
  • 61–100 (Red): Pattern matches manipulated profiles. Pause new exact-match anchors immediately; audit lowest-quality referring domains for disavow candidates.

What This Tool Does NOT Do

Anchor distribution is one input, not the whole story. The analyzer cannot see:

  • Quality of the linking pages (DR, traffic, topical fit)
  • Whether the anchor is wrapped in rel=nofollow or rel=sponsored
  • Page-level vs domain-level anchor concentration
  • Velocity (how fast anchors were acquired)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a healthy exact-match anchor ratio?

For most niches keep exact-match anchors under 5–8% of your total backlink profile. Above 15% is the classic Penguin red zone where algorithmic devaluation typically kicks in.

How many anchors should I analyze?

Paste at least 50 anchors for a meaningful distribution. Under 30 the percentages swing wildly with each new link, so the risk score becomes noisy.

Does the tool send my data anywhere?

No. The analyzer runs 100% in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, stored, or logged. Refresh the page and your list is gone.

What counts as a branded anchor?

Any anchor containing your brand keyword (which you enter in the brand field). Variants like "YourBrand", "YourBrand.com", "team at YourBrand" all classify as branded.

Why is naked URL anchor important?

Naked URLs (https://yoursite.com) are the most natural anchor pattern in the wild. Profiles with 0% naked URLs often look manipulated to algorithmic classifiers.
